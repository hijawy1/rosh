A 43-year-old previously healthy woman complains of experiencing sudden-onset shortness of breath and dyspnea. She reports passing out twice with exertion in the last week. A CT of the chest reveals an invasive mass in the left atrium and no other significant findings. Which of the following is the most likely cause of her shortness of breath?

  • ACardiac metastases from the lung
  • BCardiac myxoma
  • CCardiac tamponade
  • DPrimary cardiac sarcomaCorrect Answer

Correct Answer ( D )

Explanation:

Primary sarcomas arising in the heart are generally rapidly progressive and cause death through infiltration of the myocardium, by obstructing circulation, or by distant metastases (most commonly to the brain). Angiosarcoma is the most common subtype of cardiac sarcomas seen on histological examination. Patients are often middle aged, with a median age in the 40s, and the disease is more common in female patients than male patients. Cardiac sarcomas are often unrecognized until the patient initially presents with dyspnea on exertion, hemoptysis, or with frank shortness of breath. When feasible, treatment is surgical, although chemotherapy and radiation therapy also have been utilized. Most of these tumors recur relatively rapidly and have a high mortality rate.



Lung cancer metastasizing to the heart (A) is a common form of cardiac metastasis, as is breast cancer and thyroid cancer, but metastatic cancers often favor either the right atrium or occur via direct invasion from the mediastinum. Furthermore, this patient has no other signs of primary tumor on CT scan. Cardiac myxomas (B) make up the majority of primary cardiac tumors and can cause syncope by obstructing the mitral valve, however, these are rarely invasive tumors. Malignant pericardial effusions are common in cardiac tumors, and can eventually lead to cardiac tamponade (C) which is life threatening, but in this case, the patient has an invasive mass of the left atrium, which is more concerning for an aggressive cardiac sarcoma.

Question: Which part of the heart do cardiac myxomas favor?

Reveal Answer Left atrium.
